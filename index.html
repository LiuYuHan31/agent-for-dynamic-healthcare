Overview

From static medical QA to governed clinical agents

AI systems for healthcare are moving from static prediction and single-turn question answering toward agents that reason over evolving clinical context, use tools, coordinate with clinicians, and support bounded actions. The workshop builds a NeurIPS community around world models of patient trajectories and care processes, simulation environments, dynamic evaluation, and human-governed deployment.

The workshop emphasizes safety, auditability, oversight, privacy, and workflow integration as prerequisites for responsible clinical use. The workshop is designed for researchers in agents, reinforcement learning, world models, foundation models, medical AI, clinical NLP, imaging, informatics, HCI, privacy, safety, and responsible deployment, along with clinicians and health-system collaborators.

Topics

Research areas of interest

Submissions should address agentic AI, clinical medicine, and healthcare systems.

Clinical AI Agents

Diagnosis, triage, documentation, monitoring, care planning, coordination, and multi-agent clinical teams.

Medical Reasoning

Longitudinal reasoning, belief updates under uncertainty, evidence use, and safe clinician deferral.

World Models

Patient trajectory models, disease simulators, digital twins, and uncertainty-aware testbeds.

Clinical Tool Use

Realistic environments where agents interact with records, retrieval systems, and ordering tools.

Dynamic Evaluation

Protocols beyond aggregate accuracy, including calibration and clinically meaningful failure modes.

Safety and Reliability

Bounded autonomy, hallucination mitigation, adversarial robustness, monitoring, and incident response.

Human-Agent Workflow

Clinician-in-the-loop design, trust, escalation, care-team coordination, and deployment studies.

Datasets and Tools

Benchmark environments, synthetic patient data, annotation protocols, reporting standards, and open simulators.

Governance and Positions

Regulatory pathways, accountability, health equity, responsible deployment, and evaluation standards.

Scope

The workshop welcomes contributions at the intersection of clinical agents, world models, patient simulation, dynamic evaluation, safety, human oversight, privacy, and workflow integration. Position papers are welcome when they identify unsolved risks or propose evaluation standards.

Participation formats

Planned formats include invited talks, contributed talks, posters, demos, benchmark presentations, a structured debate, a panel, breakout discussions, lightning talks, and mentoring-oriented reviews.

Reviewing policy

The workshop will follow NeurIPS workshop conflict-of-interest requirements. Organizers will not submit papers, conflicted submissions will be excluded from organizer assessment, and each submission is expected to receive at least three reviews. Reviewers will assess technical quality, clinical relevance, safety implications, and fit to the workshop theme.
